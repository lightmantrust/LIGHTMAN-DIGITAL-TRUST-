### ARTICLE 2: DECLARATION OF TRUST

**2.1 Creation of Trust**

The Settlor hereby irrevocably settles upon the Trustee the Initial Trust Fund described in Schedule A to be held by the Trustee upon the trusts and subject to the powers and provisions of this Deed.

**2.2 Irrevocability**

This Trust is irrevocable and may not be revoked, amended, or terminated by the Settlor. The Settlor retains no right, title, or interest in the Trust Fund except as expressly provided herein.

**2.3 Settlor's Exclusion**

The Settlor shall not be a beneficiary of this Trust and shall have no beneficial interest in the Trust Fund. No distribution may be made to the Settlor under any circumstances.

**2.4 Acceptance of Additional Property**

The Trustee may accept additional property transferred to the Trust by the Settlor or any other person. Upon acceptance, such property shall become part of the Trust Fund and subject to all provisions of this Deed.

### ARTICLE 3: BENEFICIAL INTERESTS

**3.1 Income Beneficiaries**

During the Trust Period, the Trustee may pay or apply the whole or any part of the Net Income of the Trust Fund to or for the benefit of such one or more of the Beneficiaries in such shares and proportions and at such times as the Trustee in its absolute discretion thinks fit.

**3.2 Accumulation of Income**

Any Net Income not distributed in any Accounts Year shall be accumulated and added to the capital of the Trust Fund.

**3.3 Principal Distributions**

The Trustee may at any time and from time to time pay or apply the whole or any part of the capital of the Trust Fund to or for the benefit of such one or more of the Beneficiaries in such shares and proportions as the Trustee in its absolute discretion thinks fit.

**3.4 Distribution Standards**

In exercising discretion regarding distributions, the Trustee shall consider:

(a) The financial needs of each Beneficiary
(b) The Beneficiary's other resources and income
(c) The Beneficiary's age, maturity, and ability to manage funds
(d) The purposes for which distribution is requested
(e) The preservation of the Trust Fund for future generations
(f) The tax consequences of distributions
(g) The Settlor's wishes as expressed in any Letter of Wishes
(h) The impact on other Beneficiaries
(i) Any other factors the Trustee deems relevant

**3.5 Spendthrift Protection**

No Beneficiary shall have the power to anticipate, assign, mortgage, pledge, or otherwise encumber their interest in the Trust. No interest of any Beneficiary shall be subject to the claims of creditors or liable to attachment, execution, or other legal process.

**3.6 Distributions to Minors**

If a Beneficiary entitled to a distribution is a minor, the Trustee may:
(a) Pay the distribution to the Beneficiary's parent or guardian
(b) Pay the distribution to a custodian under the Uniform Transfers to Minors Act
(c) Hold the distribution in a separate sub-trust for the Beneficiary
(d) Apply the distribution directly for the Beneficiary's benefit

**3.7 Termination and Final Distribution**

Upon the expiration of the Trust Period (if any) or upon earlier termination as provided in this Deed, the Trustee shall distribute the Trust Fund to the then-living Beneficiaries in equal shares per stirpes, or as otherwise directed by the Trustee in accordance with this Deed.

### ARTICLE 4: INVESTMENT POWERS

**4.1 General Investment Power**

The Trustee shall have the full power to invest and reinvest the Trust Fund in any form of real or personal property, including but not limited to:

(a) Stocks, bonds, and securities (public or private)
(b) Shares in private companies (including Portfolio Companies)
(c) Real estate (residential, commercial, or undeveloped)
(d) Partnership interests and LLC membership interests
(e) Trusts and collective investment vehicles
(f) Commodities and precious metals
(g) Foreign investments
(h) Digital assets and cryptocurrencies (subject to limitations in Investment Policy Statement)
(i) Life insurance policies
(j) Loans (secured or unsecured)
(k) Options, futures, and derivatives
(l) Any other property of any kind

**4.2 Investment Standard**

The Trustee shall invest and manage the Trust Fund as a prudent investor would, considering:
- The purposes and terms of the Trust
- The needs of Beneficiaries
- The circumstances of the Trust
- Diversification
- Risk and return objectives
- Liquidity needs
- Tax consequences
- The role each investment plays in the overall portfolio

**4.3 No Duty to Diversify**

Notwithstanding the general duty to diversify, the Trustee may:
(a) Retain concentrated positions in Portfolio Companies
(b) Hold illiquid investments for extended periods
(c) Make investments that would not be prudent in isolation but are appropriate in the context of the Trust's purposes

**4.4 Business Operations**

The Trustee may:
(a) Engage in business operations through the Trust or through entities owned by the Trust
(b) Own 100% or any lesser percentage of corporations, LLCs, or partnerships
(c) Exercise all rights and powers of a shareholder or partner
(d) Vote shares and make decisions affecting Portfolio Companies
(e) Appoint directors and officers of Portfolio Companies
(f) Cause Portfolio Companies to enter into contracts and transactions
(g) Approve strategic plans and budgets
(h) Authorize borrowing and lending
(i) Declare dividends or make other distributions

**4.5 Delegation of Investment Management**

The Trustee may delegate investment management to:
(a) Professional investment advisors
(b) Portfolio managers
(c) Financial institutions
(d) Specialist advisors for particular asset classes

Any such delegation shall be on reasonable terms and subject to appropriate oversight by the Trustee.

**4.6 Related Party Investments**

The Trustee may:
(a) Purchase assets from the Settlor, Beneficiaries, or other related parties at fair market value
(b) Make loans to Beneficiaries on reasonable terms
(c) Lease property to or from Beneficiaries on market terms
(d) Enter into transactions with Portfolio Companies on arms-length terms

All such transactions must be fair to the Trust and properly documented.

**4.7 Borrowing**

The Trustee may borrow money for Trust purposes and pledge Trust assets as security, provided such borrowing is prudent considering the Trust's circumstances.
